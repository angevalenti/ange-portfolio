Overview

Learn by doing, not by clicking next.

Study Buddies started from a simple idea: kids learn science better when they can interact with it, not just read about it. Each lesson is one HTML file. No logins, no installations, no LMS. Open it in a browser and go. Right now the series covers two body systems — circulatory and respiratory — with animated diagrams, drag-and-drop activities, quizzes, and sound effects built in. Each lesson has its own look and feel, but they share the same level of depth and interactivity.

The Challenge

Complex systems, young learners.

Blood flow and gas exchange are invisible. You can't see them happening, which makes them abstract for ten-year-olds. Most textbooks handle this with labeled diagrams — fine for memorization, not great for actual understanding. I wanted to make the invisible visible without dumbing down the science. Every interactive element had to teach something real. The drag-and-drop isn't a game for the sake of it — it's checking whether the student understood the sequence. The animated heart isn't decoration — it's showing which direction blood flows, something a static picture just can't do.

Approach

Teach first, then play.

Introduce, then interact
Each lesson follows the same rhythm: introduce the concept, let students explore it visually, guide them through an interaction, then check understanding.
Every interaction teaches
A clickable 4-chamber heart, a 7-stop oxygen journey, drag-and-match quizzes — nothing is decorative. Every element reinforces the science.
Single-file, anywhere
Each lesson runs from one HTML file. A teacher can save it to a USB drive and use it on any device — online or off, no setup required.
