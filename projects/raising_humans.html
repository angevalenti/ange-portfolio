Overview

What parents actually
need to know.

There's no shortage of opinions about kids and screens. There's a shortage of clear, research-backed guidance that doesn't make parents feel guilty or overwhelmed. Raising Humans breaks the topic into eight manageable units — covering brain development, attention, social media mechanics, digital literacy, and practical family strategies. The course is designed to feel like a conversation, not a lecture. It draws from developmental psychology and media research, but the tone stays accessible and honest throughout.

The Challenge

Science without
the scare tactics.

Most content about kids and technology falls into two camps: either it's alarmist or dismissive. Neither is helpful. The challenge was presenting real research — some of it genuinely concerning — without triggering the guilt and anxiety that makes parents tune out. The course needed to inform and empower, not frighten.

Process Visual
Approach

Eight units.
One honest conversation.

Each unit follows a consistent structure: introduce the concept with a real-world scenario, present the research clearly, then offer practical takeaways parents can actually use. The visual design is inspired by Masterclass — clean, editorial, and respectful of the audience's intelligence. Five custom SVG infographics make complex research feel approachable without oversimplifying it.
