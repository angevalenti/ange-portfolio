Overview

Built for the classroom.
Designed for everyone.

Ready for What's Next is a 5-unit high school ESL curriculum covering digital literacy, AI tools, financial basics, workplace communication, and college/career readiness. Every unit is WIDA-differentiated across all 5 proficiency levels — so every student in the room gets the same lesson, at the right entry point.

The Challenge

WIDA-aligned content
shouldn't mean five different lessons.

High school ELL students are often handed worksheets while their peers use polished digital tools. The goal was to build something that felt like a real career resource — not a school handout — while meeting every learner where they are. One file. Five levels. No compromises on design.

Approach

Five levels.
One color system.

Each WIDA proficiency level gets its own color — not arbitrarily, but grounded in the WIDA Can-Do Descriptors for Grades 9–12. Color isn't decoration here; it's a navigation system. Students and teachers can locate the right entry point at a glance, and every activity, scaffold, and prompt is coded to match. The result is a single lesson that holds five differentiated pathways without ever feeling fragmented.
