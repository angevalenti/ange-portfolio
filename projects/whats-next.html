Overview

Future thinking,
in their own words.

A browser-based ESL module for intermediate-level secondary ELLs. Students build the language of future thinking — goal-setting, sequencing, expressing hopes and intentions — through scaffolded activities and a culminating reflection that asks for something real.

The Challenge

Enough English
to get by. Not enough
to dream out loud.

At WIDA Levels 3–4, students can navigate daily life but struggle to express complex, forward-looking thought. The language of planning — conditionals, future modals, expressed intention — is exactly where that gap shows up. Existing materials offered grammar drills or open-ended prompts, rarely both. This lesson had to occupy the space between.

Approach

Ask something real.
Get something real.

The lesson opens with the question directly — not as a grammar prompt but as a genuine invitation. Vocabulary and sentence frames are embedded in the activity flow where they're needed. Scaffolds release gradually across the lesson. The final screen holds a single open field: one real next step, in the student's own words.
