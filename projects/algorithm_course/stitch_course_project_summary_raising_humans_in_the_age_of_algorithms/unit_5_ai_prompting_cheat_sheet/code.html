Unit 5 Framework

The Role-Task-Format Framework

Turn your AI into a personalized tutor, a creative coach, or a career mentor with this 3-step structured prompting method.

How to structure your prompts

1

Define the Role

Tell the AI who it should be. "Act as a patient middle school math teacher" or "You are a playful storyteller for 5-year-olds."

2

State the Task

Be specific about the action. "Explain photosynthesis" or "Create a list of 5 dinner recipes based on these ingredients."

3

Specify the Format

How should the answer look? "A bulleted list," "a rhyming poem," "a structured table," or "a 200-word summary."

Prompts by Age Group

child_care

Early Childhood (Ages 3-6)

"Act as a friendly character. Tell a 2-minute bedtime story about a dinosaur who learns to share. Use simple words and include 3 pauses for interaction."
school

Middle Years (Ages 7-12)

"You are a fun science tutor. Explain the concept of gravity using a Minecraft analogy. Provide a table of 'low-gravity' versus 'high-gravity' planets."
psychology_alt

Adolescents (Ages 13+)

"Act as a career mentor. Based on my interest in digital art and math, suggest 5 modern career paths. Format the output as a comparative pros/cons list."

warning What to Avoid

  • ✕

    Vague Instructions

    "Help me with parenting" is too broad. Specify the struggle.

  • ✕

    Ignoring Fact-Checking

    AI can hallucinate dates and facts. Always verify school material.

  • ✕

    Over-Reliance

    The AI is a tool, not the teacher. Keep the human connection central.

  • ✕

    Data Privacy

    Never input your child's full name, address, or sensitive ID info.

lightbulb

Pro Tip: Iterate

If the first answer isn't perfect, don't start over. Just tell the AI: "That's good, but make it shorter and more humorous."
