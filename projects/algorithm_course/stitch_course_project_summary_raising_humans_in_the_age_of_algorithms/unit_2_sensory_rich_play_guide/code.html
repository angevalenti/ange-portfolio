Developmental Milestones

Infants & Toddlers (0-2 yrs)

texture
Focus: Touch

Textured Exploration Box

Fill a shallow box with silk scarves, corrugated cardboard, and smooth stones. Encourage baby to reach and grasp different tactile surfaces.

visibility
Focus: Sight

High-Contrast Shadow Play

Use a flashlight and simple cutouts to project shapes on the wall. Move them slowly to help baby develop visual tracking skills.

hearing
Focus: Sound

Homemade Rhythm Shakers

Fill sealed containers with rice, beans, or pasta. Explore different tempos and volumes together to build auditory discrimination.

Preschoolers (3-5 yrs)

palette
Focus: Complex Touch

Nature Dough Sculpting

Combine homemade salt dough with twigs, leaves, and pinecones. Pressing patterns into dough builds fine motor strength and creativity.

search
Focus: Visual Search

Color Hunt Scavenger

Give your child a specific color card. They must find 5 items in the room or backyard that exactly match that hue. Enhances focus.

music_note
Focus: Sound Mapping

Sound Scape Drawing

Sit quietly for 2 minutes. Ask the child to draw the sounds they hear (birds, cars, wind). Great for mindfulness and listening.

lightbulb

The Golden Rule of Sensory Play

Follow your child's lead. Sensory stimulation should be engaging, not overwhelming. If they pull away or seem agitated, simplify the activity or move to a quieter space. The goal is joyful discovery, not a completed project.
