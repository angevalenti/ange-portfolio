Unit 3: The Elementary Years Lesson 2 of 10
Core Module

Digital Literacy Foundations

At age 10, children move from passive consumption to active exploration. Understanding the machinery behind the screen is their first line of defense.

Evaluating Information

The elementary years are the "Golden Window" for critical thinking. As children start using search engines for school projects and curiosity, they must learn that being "first on Google" doesn't mean "most accurate."

lightbulb

Key Concept: The Algorithm Gap

Algorithms aren't truth-seekers; they are attention-seekers. Teach your child that the internet shows them what it thinks they want to see, not necessarily what is objectively true.

Safe Search Habits

Setting up technical guardrails is only step one. The real goal is "internalized safety"—the child's own ability to spot a red flag. This involves recognizing sponsored content, clickbait headlines, and suspicious redirects.

search

Side-by-Side Search

Sit with them while they research. Ask: "Why do you think this result is at the top?"

policy

The 'Silly' Check

Encourage them to check three different sources if something sounds too good (or too weird) to be true.
