The Algorithm Gap
Conversation Guide

01

info What is the Gap?

"The Algorithm Gap refers to the disconnect between the content kids see and what they actually value. It’s the digital space where engagement tactics override intentional choice."

Think of it as the difference between being served a nutritious meal you asked for versus being trapped in an endless buffet of candy because you once looked at a lollipop. The goal of this conversation is to help your child recognize when they are no longer in control of their own attention.

Spotting the Hook

A checklist for kids to identify engagement tactics used by platforms to keep them scrolling.

check
Infinite Scroll: There is no "end" or page number, so your brain doesn't get a natural stopping point.
Auto-Play: The next video starts before you've even decided if you liked the last one.
Rage Baiting: Showing you something that makes you angry because "angry people" stay on the app longer.
Variable Rewards: Using "likes" and "notifications" like a slot machine to keep you checking back.

Parental Pro-Tip

Instead of taking the phone away immediately, try "Co-Scrolling" for 10 minutes. Ask them to explain why they think the algorithm showed them a specific video. Turn it into a game of "Spot the Tactic."
