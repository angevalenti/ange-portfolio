Document Everything

Immediately take clear screenshots of all offensive messages, posts, comments, and timestamps. Do not delete evidence, even if it is painful to look at.

  • • Save to a secure, password-protected folder
  • • Include usernames and profile URLs
2
do_not_disturb_on

Do Not Retaliate

Engagement is oxygen for harassers. Instruct your child not to respond. Responding can often make the victim look like a co-aggressor in the eyes of platforms.

  • • Close the app or put away the device
  • • Remind your child: "This is not your fault."
3
shield_person

Block & Report

Use the platform's native tools to restrict access. Report the specific content for harassment to ensure the algorithm flag's the attacker's behavior.

  • • Use "Mute" for subtle distancing
  • • Use "Block" for total severance
4
gavel

Escalation

If the harasser is a schoolmate, contact school administrators. If there are threats of physical harm, involve local law enforcement immediately.

  • • Bring your documented evidence
  • • Request a written summary of the meeting
lightbulb
Parent Pro-Tip

The "Digital Sunset" Strategy

Research shows that cyberbullying feels 4x more intense at night. If an incident occurs, implement a "Digital Sunset" where all devices are kept in a common area overnight. This physical distance provides the psychological safety needed to process the situation calmly.
