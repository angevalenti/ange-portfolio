Unit 5: College & Career Portals

Career Portal
Navigation Guide

A step-by-step walkthrough of the platforms that matter most.

Between FAFSA, Common App, LinkedIn, Indeed, and your school's own college portal, the number of platforms you need to manage senior year is overwhelming. This guide walks you through the five most important ones — what they are, what to do first, and the mistakes to avoid.

1

FAFSA (Free Application for Federal Student Aid)

📋
What it is

The federal form that determines how much financial aid you qualify for. Every college-bound student should fill this out, even if you think you won't qualify.

✅
Do this first

Create an FSA ID for yourself AND have a parent/guardian create one too. You'll both need them to sign the form electronically.

⚠ Common Mistake

Waiting until the deadline. FAFSA opens October 1 — some aid is first-come, first-served. Don't leave money on the table.

2

Common App / Coalition App

📋
What it is

The main application platform for most 4-year colleges. One application, submitted to multiple schools.

✅
Do this first

Fill out the "Profile" and "Education" sections completely. These don't change between schools and save you hours later.

⚠ Common Mistake

Using the same personal essay for every school without tailoring it. Admissions officers can tell when it's generic.

3

LinkedIn

📋
What it is

Your professional profile. Think of it as a living resume that recruiters, colleges, and future employers can find.

✅
Do this first

Upload a clear, professional headshot. Write a headline that says more than "Student" — try "High School Senior | Aspiring [Your Interest] | Class of 2026".

⚠ Common Mistake

Leaving your profile blank and expecting connections to come to you. LinkedIn rewards people who post, comment, and engage.

4

Indeed / Handshake

📋
What it is

Job search platforms. Indeed is general; Handshake is specifically for students and recent grads, often connected to your school.

✅
Do this first

Upload your resume as a PDF. Set up job alerts for 2–3 roles you're interested in so new listings come to your inbox.

⚠ Common Mistake

Applying to 50 jobs with the same generic resume. Tailor your resume to each role — even small tweaks to the summary and skills section make a difference.

5

Your School's College & Career Portal

📋
What it is

Most high schools have a Naviance, Scoir, or similar platform. This is where you track applications, request transcripts, and get recommendations.

✅
Do this first

Request recommendation letters at least 3 weeks before your earliest deadline. Teachers are busy — give them time.

⚠ Common Mistake

Ignoring this portal until December. Deadlines sneak up, and your counselor needs time to process everything.

🏆 Golden Rule
The Golden Rule of Portal Management

Create one master document with every username, password, and deadline across all platforms. Update it weekly. The students who stay organized are the ones who don't miss opportunities.
