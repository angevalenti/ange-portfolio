Respond to this prompt:What advice is Chris giving his son? Do you think this advice is realistic? Can anyone achieve their dream if they work hard enough — or does luck, money, or privilege also matter?
Critical Response:This quote is often cited as inspiring and motivational. However, some critics argue that this message oversimplifies the role of systemic inequality and places too much burden on the individual. Do you agree? Use examples from the film AND from real life to support your position.
"This part of my life... this little part... is called happiness."

Discuss:Why does Chris only call "this part" happiness? What does that word "little" suggest about his journey? Does the ending feel earned?
Analyze:The film's title spells "happiness" incorrectly. The final line refers only to a "little part" of his life. Analyze how these choices — in both the title and the final narration — function as a commentary on the American Dream and whether true happiness was achieved.
