Section 01

Cells — The Building Blocks 🔬

Everything in your body starts with the tiniest unit of life — the cell. Cells group together to form tissues, tissues form organs, and organs work together as organ systems.

🔵

Cell

The smallest basic unit of all living things. Your body has about 75 trillion of them!

🧱

Tissue

A group of similar cells working together to do the same job — like muscle tissue.

❤️

Organ

Two or more types of tissue working together — like your heart, lungs, or stomach.

🌐

Organ System

A group of organs working together for a big job — like the circulatory system.

Section 02

4 Types of Human Tissue 🧬

Click each card to reveal what that tissue type does. Find all four!

ZAP!
💪

Muscle Tissue

✅
Makes your body move! Found in your arms, legs, heart, and even your digestive organs. Three types: skeletal, cardiac, and smooth muscle tissue.
⚡

Nerve Tissue

✅
Sends electrical signals through your body like high-speed wires! Found in your brain, spinal cord, and nerves. Lets you think, feel, and react.
🔗

Connective Tissue

✅
Holds everything together! Includes bone, cartilage, blood, and fat. It supports, protects, and connects your body's other tissues and organs.
🛡️

Epithelial Tissue

✅
Lines the surfaces of your blood vessels and organs, and protects your tissues. Your skin is epithelial tissue — the body's first line of defense!

Section 03

History of Cells 📜

Scientists have been studying cells for hundreds of years. Here's how we discovered them!

1665
Robert Hooke

Used a microscope to observe thin slices of cork and saw tiny box-like compartments. He called them "cells" because they reminded him of the small rooms monks lived in.

~1670s
Antonie van Leeuwenhoek

Built better microscopes and was the first to observe living microorganisms — tiny creatures he called "animalcules." He opened the door to microbiology!

1831
Robert Brown

Discovered the nucleus inside plant cells and determined it was a key structure that contains the cell's DNA — the instruction manual for life.

1838
Matthias Schleiden

Concluded that every plant is made of cells. This was the first major step toward building a complete theory of life.

1839
Theodor Schwann

Extended Schleiden's idea to animals, concluding that all animals are also made of cells. Together with Schleiden, he formed the foundation of Cell Theory.

1855
Rudolf Virchow

Added the third key idea: all cells come from other cells (Omnis cellula e cellula). New cells are always made by existing cells dividing — not from scratch!

📖 The Cell Theory

  1. All living things are made of one or more cells.
  2. The cell is the basic unit of structure and function in living things.
  3. All cells come from existing cells.

Section 05

The Circulatory System ❤️

The circulatory system is your body's delivery service — working 24/7 to bring oxygen and nutrients to every single cell and carry waste products away.

PUMP!
❤️

The Heart

A powerful muscle about the size of your fist. It beats around 100,000 times a day, pumping blood through a network of blood vessels throughout your entire body.

🩸

The Blood

The transport fluid of the body — carries oxygen, nutrients, hormones, and waste products. About 8% of your total body weight is blood!

🛤️

Blood Vessels

The "roads" of your circulatory system — over 60,000 miles of arteries, veins, and capillaries that would circle Earth more than twice!

Section 06

Blood Vessels 🛤️

There are three types of blood vessels, each with a different job in the circulatory highway.

WHOOSH!
🔴

Arteries

WHOOSH!

Carry blood AWAY from the heart. They have thick, muscular walls to handle the high pressure. The aorta is the largest artery — as wide as a garden hose!

Arteries carry oxygen-rich blood (except the pulmonary artery).

🟣

Capillaries

The tiniest blood vessels — so small that red blood cells must pass through in single file!

"10 capillaries barely as thick as one hair!" They're where oxygen and nutrients actually pass into cells.

🔵

Veins

Carry blood TOWARD the heart. They have thin walls and special valves that act like one-way doors — keeping blood moving the right direction.

Veins carry oxygen-depleted blood back to the heart (except pulmonary veins).

❤️ LUB-DUB!

That heartbeat sound? LUB is the sound of the mitral and tricuspid valves closing. DUB is the aortic and pulmonary valves closing. Two sounds, one beat!

Section 08

How Blood Flows 🔄

Follow blood on its journey through your body! Click NEXT STEP to trace the path.

1

Blood enters the Right Atrium

Oxygen-poor blood from your body flows through large veins (the vena cava) into the Right Atrium — the heart's collecting chamber on the right side.

2

Right Ventricle → Lungs

Blood drops down into the Right Ventricle, which pumps it through the pulmonary artery to the lungs — where it drops off carbon dioxide and picks up fresh oxygen.

3

Returns to Left Atrium

Oxygen-rich blood travels back from the lungs through the pulmonary veins into the Left Atrium — ready for the final push to the body.

4

Left Ventricle → Entire Body!

The Left Ventricle — the heart's strongest chamber — squeezes hard to pump oxygen-rich blood through the aorta to your entire body. Every cell gets what it needs!

🔄

And then it starts ALL OVER AGAIN!

This whole circuit happens with every single heartbeat — about 70 times per minute at rest. In your lifetime, your heart will beat over 2.5 billion times!
