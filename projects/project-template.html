Overview

One lesson.
Every learner.

An interactive ESL lesson for secondary ELLs — scaffolded across three WIDA proficiency tiers so every student engages with the same rigorous content at their level. No LMS, no login. Runs entirely in the browser.

The Challenge

One room.
Five reading levels.

In a typical ESL class, students span four or five WIDA levels simultaneously. Traditional differentiation — separate worksheets, separate pacing — signals to lower-level students that they're getting less. The goal was one experience that felt designed for everyone.

Approach

Design first.
Code second.

Each tier was mapped to WIDA Can-Do Descriptors before a line of HTML was written. Scaffolds — vocabulary banks, sentence frames, visual anchors — appear where the task demands them and taper off as proficiency rises. Same visual language across all tiers; different entry points, same content dignity.
